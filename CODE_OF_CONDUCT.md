# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers of NyayaSankalan pledge to make participation in our project and community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and professional environment where diverse perspectives are valued and respected.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- **Be Respectful**: Treat everyone with dignity and respect
- **Be Inclusive**: Welcome and support people of all backgrounds
- **Be Collaborative**: Work together towards common goals
- **Be Professional**: Maintain professional communication at all times
- **Be Constructive**: Provide helpful feedback and accept it gracefully
- **Show Empathy**: Consider others' perspectives and circumstances
- **Give Credit**: Acknowledge others' contributions and work
- **Stay Focused**: Keep discussions relevant to the project goals

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- **Harassment**: Unwelcome comments, personal attacks, or intimidation
- **Discrimination**: Prejudiced actions or remarks based on protected characteristics
- **Trolling**: Deliberately inflammatory or off-topic comments
- **Doxxing**: Publishing others' private information without consent
- **Spam**: Excessive self-promotion or irrelevant content
- **Disrespect**: Dismissive or belittling comments about others' skills or contributions
- **Disruption**: Intentionally derailing discussions or creating conflicts
- **Inappropriate Content**: Sexual, violent, or otherwise offensive material

## Project-Specific Guidelines

Given that NyayaSankalan deals with legal and justice system matters:

### Sensitivity & Confidentiality

- **Respect Privacy**: Never share real case data or personal information
- **Maintain Confidentiality**: Don't discuss sensitive legal matters publicly
- **Use Test Data**: Always use dummy/seed data in examples and discussions
- **Be Mindful**: Legal processes can be sensitive topics; discuss professionally

### Technical Discussions

- **Focus on Code**: Keep debates technical, not personal
- **Explain Clearly**: Not everyone has the same background or experience
- **Assume Good Intent**: People may make mistakes; respond constructively
- **Document Decisions**: Explain the reasoning behind technical choices

### Cultural Sensitivity

- **Respect Diversity**: India is diverse; be respectful of different perspectives
- **Language**: English is the primary language, but be patient with non-native speakers
- **Time Zones**: Contributors may be in different time zones; be understanding
- **Festivals & Holidays**: Be aware that people have different schedules

## Responsibilities

### Maintainers

Project maintainers are responsible for:

- Clarifying standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate and fair corrective action
- Removing, editing, or rejecting contributions that violate this Code
- Communicating reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate
- Setting a positive example in their own behavior

### Contributors

All contributors are responsible for:

- Reading and following this Code of Conduct
- Reporting violations they witness or experience
- Supporting others who are being treated poorly
- Helping maintain a positive community environment

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it:

1. **Email**: conduct@nyayasankalan.example.com
2. **Private Message**: Contact project maintainers directly on GitHub
3. **Anonymous Report**: Use [form link if available]

All reports will be handled confidentially.

### What to Include

When reporting, please provide:

- Your contact information (if not anonymous)
- Names/handles of people involved
- Description of the incident
- Context and any relevant screenshots/logs
- Whether the incident is ongoing
- Any other relevant information

### Response Process

1. **Acknowledgment**: Reports acknowledged within 48 hours
2. **Investigation**: Maintainers review the situation
3. **Discussion**: Involved parties may be contacted for clarification
4. **Decision**: Maintainers determine appropriate action
5. **Follow-up**: Reporter informed of outcome (if not anonymous)

### Enforcement Actions

Depending on severity, actions may include:

#### 1. Warning
- **Trigger**: First-time minor offense
- **Action**: Private written warning with explanation
- **Follow-up**: No further action if behavior improves

#### 2. Temporary Ban
- **Trigger**: Repeated minor offenses or single moderate offense
- **Action**: Temporary suspension from project participation
- **Duration**: Typically 7-30 days
- **Reinstatement**: After demonstrating understanding and commitment to change

#### 3. Permanent Ban
- **Trigger**: Severe offense or pattern of violations
- **Action**: Permanent removal from all project spaces
- **Scope**: GitHub, Discord, email lists, etc.
- **Appeals**: May be submitted after 6 months with evidence of changed behavior

### Factors Considered

When determining enforcement action:

- Severity and impact of the behavior
- Whether it was a first offense or repeated pattern
- Intent (accidental vs. malicious)
- Willingness to acknowledge and correct behavior
- History of positive contributions

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including:

- GitHub repository (issues, PRs, discussions)
- Project communication channels (Discord, Slack, email)
- Community events (meetups, conferences)
- Social media when representing the project
- Public and private interactions related to the project
